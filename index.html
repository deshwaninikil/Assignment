About

Future of Money

The ET Future of Money Summit will serve as a definitive forum on the changing face of money and the disruptions powering India’s transition to a digital, cashless economy in the future. As an ET.com initiative to explore the disruptive changes and innovations that are redefining the role and scope of money, the Summit will also educate and empower participants on the resulting economic, social, and technological impact of this transformation to a digital economy.

Indeed, the new wave of fintech innovations will have broad-reaching implications not only in the way we use and interact with money, but also reshape the way organizations and ordinary people do business in the future. The ET Future of Money Summit will bring together industry leaders from the BFSI sector, fintech startups and challenger brands, as well as policymakers, corporates, regulators, and investors who are at the forefront of innovations redefining the role and scope of money for deliberations and insights on shaping the future of finance.

They will also engage in the most definitive conversations and collaborations to equip businesses and people to navigate the major transformation unfolding in the world of finance in an increasingly digital economy.

Industry Overview

  • The world of finance is on the cusp of a major revolution that will transform the way we define, use, and manage a fundamental, everyday tool: money
  • The new wave of fintech innovations and disruptive changes will create broad-reaching implications, not only in the way we use money but also in reshaping the way organisations and ordinary people do business
  • India is already at the forefront of financial innovation and its digital tech infrastructure already serves as a model for countries across the world for fueling financial innovation and inclusion for over a billion users
  • Now, new financial technologies and rapid tech-based innovations – fueled by a robust ecosystem of Indian product and tech innovators and startups – are fast ushering in a new era of public and private digital currencies across global economies
  • India is poised to launch its own CBDC – central bank digital currency – in 2022, laying the foundations for a truly decentralized finance and fundamentally rewriting the very definition and scope of money
